My name is Jason and I'm a computer programmer living in Vancouver, British Columbia.

I've spent over ten years of my career working to produce outstanding user interfaces for big budget video games. Samples of my work are showcased below.

I often do consulting or short term contracts to help build UI solutions for a variety of clients large and small. Let me know if you could use some tech help. Even if I am not available, I may be able to refer you to a colleague.

I also do hobby programming including various demos and game jam projects. It is a great way to make new connections and the only effective way to keep my skills current. I've participated in both Ludum Dare and Global Game Jam.

For a while I was part of a game jam team that I co-founded. We called oursleves the Ghost Pixels and we produced a few small Flash games. Our most ambitious title is Black Square which we released as a free game for the Ouya, an Android OS micro-console.

One of my more popular jam projects is Many Ninjas, which has been featured on various blogs and sites dedicated to incremental progress idle games (eg. Almost Idle.) I made it in a single, lazy weekend for Ludum Dare 30. Thousands of people have played it, and a few of those people even enjoyed it.

Many of my jam projects were built for Flash or the Unity Web Player and are no longer conveniently playable on the web. Application platforms are a moving target.
